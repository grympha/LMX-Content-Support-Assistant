# Impact of Deleting Devices
### Overview
Deleting a device from the CMS is a permanent action that affects both visibility in the dashboard and access to historical records. This page outlines the consequences and provides guidance to avoid accidental data loss.
### Key Impacts
#### Permanent Removal
- The device will be permanently deleted from the CMS platform.
- It will no longer appear in the Device Manager or Dashboard Views.
#### Loss of Historical Data
- All historical playlogs, proof-of-play reports, and playback metrics tied to the device will be permanently lost.
- This includes:
Content delivery records
Ad playback evidence for audits
Reporting data for clients or internal analysis
#### Analytics Impact
- Any campaign reporting or insight dashboards referencing the deleted device will no longer retrieve its data.
- This can affect ROI tracking, campaign performance history, and compliance documentation.
### Best Practice Recommendation
If you are not 100% sure whether the device should be removed permanently, consider the following safer alternatives:
#### 1. Mark as "Archived"
- Retain the device in the system without affecting campaign planning.
- Suitable for devices that are temporarily offline or under maintenance.
#### 2. Deactivate Instead of Deleting
- Prevents the device from receiving new content while preserving historical data and playlogs.
- Can be reactivated anytime if needed.
### Who Should Perform This?
Only Administrators or users with Device Management permissions should perform deletions after proper review with:
- Campaign Managers
- Operation Team
- Compliance or Audit stakeholders (if applicable)
